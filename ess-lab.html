Interdisciplinary Strategies for Environmental Systems & Societies (2026 Syllabus)

🌍 Inquiry & Research Question (Crit A)
Environmental Context

The Local-Global Bridge

Don't just pick a science topic; find an environmental issue and bridge it to a local study.

Strategy: Start with a Global Issue (e.g., Eutrophication) → Link to a Local Site (e.g., Lake Lugano) → Formulate RQ: "To what extent does the nitrate concentration ($ppm$) affect the dissolved oxygen levels ($mg/L$) in Lake Lugano?"
✓ Quality Checklist:
  • Includes a specific environmental issue in the introduction.
  • RQ includes IV, DV, and units.
  • Clearly identifies your Environmental Value System (EVS) perspective.
⚖️ Strategy & Tensions (Crit B - NEW)
Societal Context

The Stakeholder Conflict Map

Identify an existing management strategy for your issue and explain why it causes tension.

Stakeholder Perspective (EVS) Goal/Tension
Local Farmers Anthropocentric Need fertilizers for high crop yields/economic survival.
Conservationists Ecocentric Protect water quality and biodiversity from runoff.
Strategy: Use terms like "Conflict of Goals" or "Trade-offs." This is where you analyze the *Societies* part of ESS.
🛠️ Methodology & Safety (Crit C)

The 5x5 Justification

Ensure you have at least 5 increments of your IV and 5 repeats for each.

Safety, Ethics & Environment (SEE):
  • Safety: Protecting the researcher (gloves, goggles).
  • Ethics: No harm to sentient animals; minimizing plant waste.
  • Environment: How did you handle waste? (e.g., "Acid neutralized before disposal").
📊 Data Treatment (Crit D)

The Standard Deviation Strategy

In ESS, variability is normal. Use Standard Deviation (SD) to show the reliability of your data.

Strategy: If your SD bars on a graph overlap, your results may not be statistically significant, even if the means are different!

📈 Analysis & Conclusion (Crit E)

Choosing Your Inferential Statistics

To reach the top marks, you must prove your results aren't just a fluke. Use this decision tree:

If your goal is to... And your data looks like... Use this test:
Find a Relationship Two sets of continuous numbers (e.g., Temp vs. Growth) Pearson or Spearman Correlation
Compare Two Sites Comparing the means of two distinct groups (e.g., Forest A vs Forest B) Unpaired T-Test
Compare Frequencies Comparing "Counted" data vs. what you expected (e.g., Species counts) Chi-Squared ($\chi^2$) Test
The "So What?" Loop: Link your conclusion back to the Environmental Value Systems discussed in Criterion A. Does your data support a Technocentric or Ecocentric solution?
🔍 Evaluation (Crit F)

Specific-Impact-Solution Loop

Avoid "human error." Analyze how specific limitations affected your data's accuracy.

Limitation Impact on Data Improvement
Small Sample Size High SD; results may not represent the whole ecosystem. Increase to 50 samples per site to normalize data.
Final Touch: Suggest a realistic modification to the *Strategy* you identified in Criterion B based on your results.
