STUDENT FIELD GUIDE

Socratic Red Teaming

Use this guide to dismantle AI-generated content and rebuild it with human expertise. Click each challenge to reveal your tactical response.

Phase 1: Basic Red Team Challenges

"The AI output looks 100% correct to me."
Strategy: Kill the Filler

AI loves "Fluency" (sounding good) over "Accuracy" (being right). Highlight every adjective and adverb the AI used. If you remove them, is there any data left?

Socratic Inquiry: "What specific evidence is this 'significant' claim based on? Can I replace this vague word with a hard number?"
"I suspect a mistake, but I'm not sure."
Strategy: The 2-Source Rule

Never trust an AI to verify itself. You must find two independent, non-AI sources (textbooks, databases, journals) that confirm the claim.

Socratic Inquiry: "If I were a critic of this information, what primary sources would I use to debunk it?"

Phase 2: Advanced Red Team Challenges

"The AI provided citations, but I can't find them."
Strategy: Spot the Citation Mirage

AI often "hallucinates" sources—it creates titles and authors that sound real but don't exist. A citation is a lie until you open the link or find the book.

Socratic Inquiry: "Why did the AI choose this specific author? Does this person actually exist and write about this topic?"
"The AI is just agreeing with everything I say."
Strategy: The Perspective Pivot

This is the "Echo Chamber." The AI is mirroring your bias back to you. You aren't learning; you're just being validated.

Socratic Inquiry: "What is the most statistically significant counter-argument to this claim? Provide a summary from the perspective of an expert who disagrees."
"The writing is so professional, I feel bad criticizing it."
Strategy: De-Style the Output

Don't be fooled by "The Paradox of Fluency." Good grammar doesn't equal truth. Rewrite the AI's response in plain, simple bullet points. Strip the 'fancy' language away to see if the logic still holds up.

Socratic Inquiry: "Is the AI using complex language to mask a lack of specific knowledge?"
"I'm starting to think the AI is wrong about EVERYTHING."
Strategy: The Green-Light Audit

Don't fall into "Hyper-Skepticism." Red Teaming is about finding the truth, not just the errors. Use your sources to find what the AI actually got right. What can you prove is a solid foundation?

Phase 3: Tactical Prompting (Rig the System)

Structure your prompts to make the AI easier to audit.

How do I get the AI to "Show its Work"?
Tactic: Chain-of-Thought (CoT)

Force the AI to explain its logic step-by-step before it gives the answer.

"Explain your reasoning for this claim step-by-step. For every step, identify one potential counter-argument."
How do I stop the AI from being a "Yes-Man"?
Tactic: Persona Priming

Tell the AI to be a skeptical peer-reviewer or a devil's advocate.

"Act as a critical editor. Your goal is to find three logical fallacies or omissions in your previous answer."
How do I prevent the AI from guessing?
Tactic: Negative Constraints

Give the AI strict rules on what it cannot do.

"Do not use generic adjectives. If you do not have a specific date, name, or statistic for a claim, state 'Data Missing' instead of generalizing."
Final Mastery: Your "Hardened Draft" submission must include:
  • The original AI output with highlighted errors/omissions.
  • The Socratic Questions you used to interrogate the text.
  • The Verified Version with 2+ cited sources.
