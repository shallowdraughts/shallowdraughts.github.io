STUDENT FIELD GUIDE

Socratic Red Teaming

Use this guide to dismantle AI-generated content and rebuild it with human expertise. Click each challenge to reveal your tactical response.

Phase 1 & 2: The Audit Challenges

The AI sounds too perfect or vague.
Tactical Move: De-Styling

Strip the grammar. Convert the AI response into simple bullet points of pure data. If the data disappears, the response was "hallucinated fluff."

The AI is providing fake citations (Mirages).
Tactical Move: The Link-Check

Verify every source. If you can't find a live DOI, a library entry, or a physical book, mark the claim as FAILED.

Phase 3: Tactical Prompting (Rig the System)

Don't just ask questions—structure your prompts to make the AI easier to audit.

How do I get the AI to "Show its Work"?
Tactic: Chain-of-Thought (CoT)

Force the AI to explain its logic step-by-step before it gives the answer. It is much easier to find an error in a 10-step list than in a paragraph.

"Explain your reasoning for this claim step-by-step. For every step, identify one potential counter-argument."
How do I stop the AI from being a "Yes-Man"?
Tactic: Persona Priming

Give the AI a role that requires critical thinking. Tell it to be a skeptical peer-reviewer or a devil's advocate.

"Act as a critical editor. Your goal is to find three logical fallacies or omissions in your previous answer."
How do I prevent the AI from guessing?
Tactic: Negative Constraints

Give the AI strict rules on what it cannot do. This sets a trap: if the AI breaks the rule, you've found a vulnerability.

"Do not use generic adjectives. If you do not have a specific date, name, or statistic for a claim, state 'Data Missing' instead of generalizing."
Final Mastery: A "Hardened Draft" includes the original AI error, your Socratic Interrogation, and your tactical prompt improvement.
