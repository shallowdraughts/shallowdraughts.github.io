⚗️ The Chemistry Research Question

Focusing Your Variables

In Chemistry, "Temperature" or "Concentration" are your best friends. Ensure your units are molar ($M$ or $mol\ dm^{-3}$).

"How does the concentration of Sodium Thiosulfate ($0.05$ to $0.25\ mol\ dm^{-3}$) affect the rate of reaction ($s^{-1}$) when reacted with $1.0\ M\ HCl$ at room temperature?"

⚠️ Safety & Environmental Impact

The MSDS Protocol

You must list specific hazards for every chemical used. Use the standard hazard symbols.

Example: "0.5M Silver Nitrate ($AgNO_3$): Oxidizer and skin corrosive. Disposal: Precipitated as $AgCl$ and collected in a heavy-metal waste container to avoid aquatic toxicity."

Connecting Mass to Moles

🔍 Evaluation: Chemistry-Specific Errors

Beyond Random Error

In Chemistry, two errors dominate: Heat Loss and Impurity.

SourceImpact on ResultImprovement
Heat loss to surroundingsEnthalpy ($\Delta H$) calculated will be lower than literature value.Use a bomb calorimeter or double-nested polystyrene cups.
Incomplete reactionYield will be lower; molar ratios will be skewed.Increase reaction time or use a catalyst.
